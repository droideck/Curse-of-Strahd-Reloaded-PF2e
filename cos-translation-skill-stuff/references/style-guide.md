# Russian style guide for the Strahd home-game

## Register

The reference points for Russian prose in this project are classical Russian gothic and 19th-century literary narrators:

- Lermontov, framing chapters of *«Герой нашего времени»* — voice of a watching narrator.
- Pushkin, *«Пиковая дама»* — taut, restrained, every sentence earns its place.
- Gogol, *«Вий»* — supernatural seen plainly, no hysterics.
- Dostoyevsky in his quieter moments — psychological without melodrama.

The register sounds like a literary narrator who has seen things and speaks plainly. Educated but not bookish. Restrained but not dry.

**Not the reference points:**
- Modern bureaucratic / business Russian («осуществить», «реализация», «в рамках выполнения»).
- Blog-Russian / colloquial («чисто», «реально», «походу»).
- LLM-Russian (calques, verbose copulas, English-shape subordination).
- Pseudo-archaic / church-Slavonic («еси», «иже», «пред лицем», «отверзлися»). The register is classical, not antiquarian.

## Sentence rhythm

- Comma-driven. Mix short clauses with longer ones.
- Parallelism welcome (anaphora, repetition of structure): *«Кто туда уходил, не возвращался; кто оттуда бежал и выжил, рассказывал…»*
- Avoid long subordinate-clause chains with «что..., который..., который...». If you reach a third subordinate, break the sentence.
- Em-dashes earn their place. A dash is dramatic. Overuse blunts.
- Semicolons join two independent thoughts: when a comma is too light and a period too heavy.

## Punctuation

- Russian em-dash «—» (U+2014) with spaces on both sides. Never hyphen-minus «-» as a dash. Never «--».
- Quotes: «»  outer, "" inner. Periods and commas go OUTSIDE the closing quote (« текст ».), not inside.
- No Oxford comma before «и» in a final list item. *«А, Б и В.»* not *«А, Б, и В.»*.
- Colon (:) introduces a list, an explanation, or direct speech.
- Semicolon (;) joins two clauses that could stand alone but belong together.
- Ellipsis «…» (U+2026) as a single character, not three dots.

## Vocabulary

- **Concrete nouns over abstract ones.** Show, don't categorize.
- **Verbs of action over verbs of state.** *«Стоит у окна»* over *«находится у окна»*.
- **Drop intensifiers** («очень», «крайне», «чрезвычайно») unless they earn their place.
- **Drop throat-clearing** («в целом», «по сути», «как бы», «в принципе»).
- **Russian word over Anglicism** when the Russian word exists:
  - **лимит** → **предел**
  - **локация** → **место**, **поселение**, **уголок**
  - **гайд** → **руководство**, **подсказка**, **подспорье**
  - **квест** → **задание**, **дело**, **испытание**
  - **сетап** → **завязка**, **установка**
  - **бэкграунд** → **прошлое**, **происхождение**, **история**
  - **апгрейд** → **улучшение**
  - **фидбек** → **отклик**, **отзыв**
  - **тренд** → **наклон**, **направление**, **мода**
- **Gaming jargon stays English** (NPC, GM, party, level, XP, Recovery, V, Milestone XP). Table convention.
- **Mechanic-as-noun** (Complication, Negotiation, Combat, Test, Project) — capitalized when referring to the Draw Steel mechanic, lowercase when used in ordinary prose meaning.

## Names and proper nouns

The authoritative list lives in [cos-glossary.md](cos-glossary.md). It covers every named character, location, faction, creature, item, and house-rules term in the campaign. **Match those spellings exactly** every time the term appears.

Key reminders pulled out:

- **Morninglord → Утренний Бог** (capital Б — *the* God, not a common noun).
- **Vistani → Вистани** (capitalised as a proper plural, like «Хранители Пера»). The peasants' word for them, used in derision, lowercases to «вистани»; the people's own name does not.
- **Order of the Silver Dragon → Орден Серебряного Дракона** (capital Д).
- **Mists → Туманы** when referring to the Mists of Ravenloft.
- **Castle Ravenloft → Замок Равенлофт** *(both capitalised — это собственное имя замка)*.
- **Barovians (gen. pl.) → баровийцев** — NOT *«барованцев»*. *«Баровийцы»* is the regular plural of *«баровиец»*.

When introducing a canonical proper noun for the first time in a session, give the English in parentheses on first mention if it might be searched: *«Янтарный храм (Amber Temple)»*. After that, Russian only.

## Capitalization

- Proper nouns and titles: capitalized.
- First word of a sentence: capitalized.
- Common nouns mid-sentence: NOT capitalized (English habit to avoid).
- Gods and deities, the Mists, specific Domains, named factions (Каргат, Орден Серебряного Дракона, Хранители Пера): capitalised.
- «Бог» in the sense of *the* God or *a specific* god: capitalised. «бог» as common noun: lowercase.
- **Class and subclass names stay English** per SKILL.md principle 6 — *«Аида — Censor»*, never *«Аида — Цензор»*. Same for Complication, Title, Kit, Ability, Ancestry names. Russian prose wraps the English term; the English term itself is invariant. See [cos-glossary.md](cos-glossary.md) for proper-noun renderings of in-fiction entities (characters, places, items) — those *do* get Russian spellings.

## Address forms

- Player handouts: «вы» (formal / plural).
- Direct address to one player (when Simon uses «ты» with them): «ты».
- NPC speech: depends on the NPC. Nobility, clerics, formal characters use «вы»; peasants, intimates, drunkards use «ты». A character switching from «вы» to «ты» mid-conversation is dramatically significant — preserve that signal.
- Match Simon's existing usage in the file. If unsure, ask.

## Numbers

- Spell out numbers from one to ten in narrative prose. Use figures from 11 on, and always for game mechanics (level 3, XP 16, V 5).
- Roman numerals for Act / Chapter labels (Act II, Chapter IV).
- Avoid English-style "10,000" — Russian uses non-breaking space «10 000» or no separator «10000» for game values.

## Gender (grammatical)

- Russian verbs and adjectives agree with subject gender. Aida is feminine: *«Аида приняла»*, not *«Аида принял»*.
- When the addressee's gender is unknown (e.g., a player whose character isn't built yet): default to masculine for verb agreement in instructional prose, OR rewrite to avoid the agreement entirely.
- When addressing both players together: plural verb forms work regardless of mixed gender.
